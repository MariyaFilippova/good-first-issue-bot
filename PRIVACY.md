# Privacy Policy for GFI Bot

**Last updated: July 14, 2026**

GFI Bot ("we", "the service") emails you when repositories you follow on GitHub
receive a new "good first issue". This policy explains what data we collect, why,
and how it is handled. It covers both the GFI Bot service at
[gfibot.com](https://gfibot.com) and the "Good First Issue Subscriber" browser
extension.

## What we collect

We collect only what is needed to deliver notifications:

- **Your GitHub account identifier and email address.** When you sign in with
  GitHub (OAuth), GitHub provides us your account ID and the email address on your
  GitHub account. We use the email address to send you notifications.
- **Your repository subscriptions.** The GitHub repositories you choose to follow.
  When you click subscribe on a repository's GitHub page, the extension reads that
  repository's owner and name (from the page you are viewing) and sends it to our
  service.
- **Notification records.** Which issues we have already emailed you about, so we
  don't notify you twice.

We do **not** collect your GitHub password (sign-in is handled by GitHub via
OAuth), your browsing history, or any data from web pages other than the
repository owner/name of a GitHub repo you explicitly choose to subscribe to.

## How we use your data

Your data is used solely to operate the service:

- To authenticate you (keep you signed in).
- To determine which repositories to poll on your behalf.
- To send you email notifications about new good-first-issues.

We do **not** sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties for
advertising or marketing.

## Third-party services

To provide the service we rely on:

- **GitHub** — for sign-in (OAuth) and to fetch public issue data from the
  repositories you follow. See the [GitHub Privacy Statement](https://docs.github.com/site-policy/privacy-policies/github-general-privacy-statement).
- **Resend** — to deliver notification emails. Your email address is shared with
  Resend for this purpose. See the [Resend Privacy Policy](https://resend.com/legal/privacy-policy).

## Cookies

We use a single session cookie to keep you signed in after you authenticate with
GitHub. It is used only for authentication and contains no tracking or advertising
identifiers.

## Data storage and security

Your data is stored in a private PostgreSQL database on our server. Access is
restricted to the operation of the service.

## Data retention and deletion

We retain your data for as long as you use the service. You can:

- **Unsubscribe from a repository** at any time via the extension, which removes
  that subscription.
- **Request deletion of your account and all associated data** by contacting us at
  the address below. On request we will delete your user record, subscriptions,
  and notification history.

## Children's privacy

GFI Bot is not directed to children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect data
from them.

## Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by
updating the "Last updated" date above.
